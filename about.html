Who we are

TIKA is a scientific initiative dedicated to the study, conservation, and appreciation of Andean plant diversity. We believe science must be accessible to everyone and that knowledge is a fundamental tool for biodiversity conservation.

Through scientific research, education, outreach, and technical services, we bring botanical knowledge closer to students, professionals, communities, and institutions, contributing to the conservation of the Andean natural heritage.

Mission

To promote research, education, and conservation of Andean plants and ecosystems, bringing science closer to people.

Vision

A future where Andean plants and ecosystems are known, valued, and conserved by society.

Our philosophy

We believe knowledge should be accessible to all. Scientific research makes sense when it helps us better understand nature, train new generations, and generate real actions for conservation.

For this reason, we promote science that is accessible, participatory, and deeply committed to biodiversity.

Team

TIKA is built by a network of researchers, students, and collaborators committed to the study and conservation of Andean biodiversity.

Our work connects field exploration, scientific research, and education to generate real conservation impact.
